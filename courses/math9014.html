CONTACT 		MATH 9014/9015: Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry

Course Description: This is a year-long course on the fundamental concepts of commutative algebra as well as classical and modern algebraic geometry. Topics for the ﬁrst semester include: ideals of commutative rings, modules, Noetherian and Artinian rings, Noether normalization, Hilbert’s Nullstellensatz, rings of fractions, primary decomposition, discrete valuation rings, elements of the dimension theory, affine and projective varieties. Topics for the second semester include: morphisms of algebraic varieties, birational equivalence, and rudiments of intersection theory. In the second semester, students will also learn about schemes, morphisms of schemes, coherent sheaves, and divisors.
